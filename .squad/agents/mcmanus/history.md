## Learnings

1. **Hook Integration** — All three provided hooks can be woven naturally through narrative rather than forced. Best approach is integrating at multiple points across docs rather than compartmentalizing.

2. **Developer-First Writing** — Developers value clarity over marketing. Balance is achieved by: (1) opening with problem/solution hook, (2) delivering immediate value (5-min quick start), (3) providing technical depth for the curious, (4) showing real business impact via case studies.

3. **Case Studies as Proof** — Concrete numbers > abstract benefits. Real scenarios (MedTech, FinServe, Open-Source) with before/after metrics more convincing than marketing claims.

4. **Agent Architecture as Differentiator** — The five-agent model (Intent, Analysis, Safety, Execution, Report) is the true innovation story. Communicating it clearly builds confidence that the system is thoughtful and safe.

5. **Safety as Feature** — Developers trust systems that have clear guardrails. Documenting dry-run, rollback, audit trails, and safety checks builds confidence to use in production.

6. **Local-First Positioning** — In era of cloud consolidation, local-first is increasingly valuable (privacy, latency, offline, cost). This is both a technical feature AND a market differentiator.
